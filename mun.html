About the Event

The Model United Nations (MUN) at ManGalaM 2026 is a simulation of the United Nations, where participants act as delegates representing different countries.

This event provides a platform for students to engage in diplomatic discussions, debate global issues, and collaboratively develop solutions to real-world problems.

Event Objectives

  • Enhance public speaking and communication skills
  • Develop critical thinking and problem-solving abilities
  • Encourage leadership and teamwork
  • Promote awareness of global issues and diplomacy

Eligibility

MGM Exclusive

This event is exclusively open to students from MGM institutions.

Individual Event

Only individual participation is allowed. No teams.

Beginners Welcome

First-time delegates and absolute beginners are highly encouraged!

Committees & Agendas

Participants will be assigned to one of the following prestigious committees:

UNGA

United Nations General Assembly

WHO

World Health Organization

UNSC

United Nations Security Council

Agendas: Releasing Soon

The official agendas for each committee will be announced shortly. Registered participants will be notified once the topics are released. Participants are encouraged to stay updated with current global affairs.

Event Format

1

Opening Session

Introduction to the committee and its agenda, followed by the official roll call of delegates.

2

Formal Debate

Delegates present their respective country’s stance with structured speeches and formal discussions.

3

Moderated Caucus

A focused discussion phase concentrating on specific subtopics of the agenda.

4

Unmoderated Caucus

Informal discussions, lobbying, and negotiations between delegates to build working blocs.

5

Resolution Drafting

Delegates collaboratively author and draft solutions and working papers into formal resolutions.

6

Voting Session

The final resolutions drafted by the blocs are formally voted upon by the committee.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Delegates must represent their assigned country seriously and professionally.
  • Formal dress code (Western Formals / Indian Formals) is absolutely mandatory.
  • Respectful behavior towards the Executive Board and other delegates must be maintained at all times.
  • Use of parliamentary language and third-person pronouns is heavily encouraged.
  • Plagiarism in position papers or misconduct during debates will lead to disqualification.
  • The decision of the Executive Board / Judges will be final and binding.
